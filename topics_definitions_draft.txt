--- Topic 1: New Venture Creation ---
Examines the multi-stage socio-technical process through which entrepreneurs identify opportunities, assemble resources, and design scalable business models to create market entries, with emphasis on the dynamic capabilities required for early-stage growth and organizational legitimacy (extending Davidsson, 2015).

--- Topic 2: Academic Spin-offs ---
Investigates the complex knowledge transfer mechanisms and institutional arrangements that enable research commercialization, analyzing how academic entrepreneurs navigate dual identities and institutional logics when translating scientific discoveries into market-based ventures (building on Shane, 2004).

--- Topic 3: Gender in Entrepreneurship ---
Analyzes the gendered nature of entrepreneurial opportunity structures, resource access, and legitimation processes, examining how socio-cultural norms and institutional arrangements reproduce or challenge systemic barriers to entrepreneurial participation across gender identities (extending Ahl, 2006).

--- Topic 4: Sustainable Innovation ---
Examines the socio-technical transition processes through which entrepreneurs develop solutions that simultaneously address ecological boundaries and economic viability, with particular focus on the governance mechanisms, stakeholder engagement strategies, and financial innovations that enable sustainability-oriented ventures (building on Schaltegger & Wagner, 2011).

--- Topic 5: High-Growth Technology Ventures ---
Investigates the distinctive organizational capabilities, resource orchestration strategies, and ecosystem dependencies that enable technology-intensive ventures to achieve exponential growth, with attention to the spatial clustering dynamics that facilitate knowledge spillovers and specialized resource access (extending Autio et al., 2014).

--- Topic 6: Economic Policy Design ---
Analyzes the intentional construction and implementation of policy instruments that shape entrepreneurial opportunity structures and resource flows, examining how fiscal, regulatory, and institutional arrangements influence the quantity, quality, and distributional impacts of entrepreneurial activity across economic systems (building on Acs et al., 2016).

--- Topic 7: Policy Innovation ---
Examines the adaptive policymaking processes through which governments develop novel approaches to entrepreneurship support, analyzing how evidence-based experimentation, stakeholder co-creation, and institutional learning enable policy systems to respond effectively to rapidly evolving business innovation trends (extending Mazzucato, 2016).

--- Topic 8: Entrepreneurship Education ---
Investigates the pedagogical frameworks, experiential methodologies, and institutional contexts that develop entrepreneurial competencies and identities, examining how educational interventions influence cognitive schemas, self-efficacy beliefs, and eventual venture creation behaviors across diverse learner populations (building on Nabi et al., 2017).

--- Topic 9: Marginalized Contexts ---
Examines entrepreneurial agency within structurally constrained environments characterized by institutional voids, resource scarcity, or social exclusion, analyzing how entrepreneurs employ bricolage, informal networks, and innovative adaptation strategies to overcome contextual limitations in underserved settings (extending Webb et al., 2020).

--- Topic 10: Startup Financing ---
Analyzes the socially embedded processes through which new ventures acquire financial resources, examining how entrepreneurs navigate investment networks, signaling mechanisms, and legitimacy-building strategies while addressing structural inequalities in capital allocation across demographic and geographic boundaries (building on Gompers & Lerner, 2001).

--- Topic 11: Global Entrepreneurship Policy ---
Examines cross-national variations in entrepreneurship policy design, implementation, and effectiveness, analyzing how institutional configurations, developmental stages, and cultural contexts moderate policy outcomes across diverse national settings while identifying mechanisms for context-sensitive policy transfer (extending Bruton et al., 2008).

--- Topic 12: Social Entrepreneurship ---
Investigates the hybrid organizational forms, governance structures, and strategic approaches through which entrepreneurs address social problems while maintaining financial viability, examining how ventures navigate the inherent tensions between social impact maximization and commercial sustainability (building on Dacin et al., 2011)[4]. --- Topic 13: SMEs Socio-economic Development ---
Analyzes the mechanisms through which small and medium enterprises contribute to economic development, job creation, and structural transformation, examining how enabling policies, financial architectures, and capacity development programs determine SME contributions to inclusive growth trajectories (extending Beck & Demirgüç-Kunt, 2006).

--- Topic 14: Family Business ---
Examines the distinctive decision-making processes, resource configurations, and intergenerational dynamics in family-controlled enterprises, analyzing how socioemotional priorities, family relationships, and governance structures influence entrepreneurial orientation and innovation capabilities (building on Chua et al., 1999).

--- Topic 15: Policy Diffusion ---
Traces the network-based mechanisms through which entrepreneurship policy innovations spread across jurisdictional boundaries, examining the role of policy entrepreneurs, knowledge brokers, and institutional isomorphism in facilitating policy learning, adaptation, and implementation within governance systems (extending Mintrom & Norman, 2009).

--- Topic 16: Entrepreneurial Ecosystems ---
Conceptualizes the interdependent networks of actors, institutions, and resources that collectively enable entrepreneurial activity within spatial boundaries, examining how ecosystem governance, relational architectures, and resource flows co-evolve to create environments that facilitate or constrain different forms of entrepreneurial activity (building on Stam & Spigel, 2017).

--- Topic 17: Entrepreneurial Finance Policy ---
Examines the deliberate policy interventions designed to address capital market failures for entrepreneurial ventures, analyzing how public financial instruments, regulatory frameworks, and institutional arrangements influence investment behaviors, risk perceptions, and resource allocation across diverse entrepreneurial activities (extending Brealey et al., 2012).

--- Topic 18: Inclusive Entrepreneurship ---
Investigates approaches to expanding entrepreneurial opportunity across demographic, socioeconomic, and spatial boundaries, examining how targeted policies, support structures, and community-based initiatives can overcome systemic exclusion and enable marginalized groups to achieve economic integration through entrepreneurship (building on Light & Dana, 2013).

--- Topic 19: Climate Technology Policy ---
Analyzes the policy frameworks that accelerate climate-responsive technological innovation, examining how regulatory incentives, public investments, and multi-stakeholder governance arrangements stimulate entrepreneurial solutions for climate challenges while navigating complex socio-technical transition pathways (extending George et al., 2016).

--- Topic 20: Digital Business Models ---
Investigates how digital technologies transform entrepreneurial opportunity creation, value capture mechanisms, and market organization, examining the emergence of platform-based business models, data-driven value propositions, and networked value creation processes that restructure entrepreneurial strategies in the digital economy (building on Nambisan, 2017)[2]. --- Topic 21: Entrepreneurial Cognition ---
Examines the distinctive mental models, heuristic reasoning processes, and cognitive frameworks that shape entrepreneurial opportunity recognition and exploitation, analyzing how socio-cultural contexts influence self-efficacy beliefs, risk perceptions, and decision-making approaches across diverse entrepreneurial populations (extending Bandura, 1997).

--- Topic 22: Diaspora Entrepreneurship ---
Analyzes the transnational entrepreneurial activities of migrant communities, examining how diaspora entrepreneurs leverage dual embeddedness, cross-border social networks, and multicultural competencies to identify opportunities and create ventures that bridge institutional environments while contributing to both host and home country development (building on Portes et al., 2002).

--- Topic 23: Low-Carbon Transition ---
Examines the entrepreneurial dynamics driving socio-technical transitions toward decarbonized economic systems, analyzing how innovative financing mechanisms, market structures, and policy frameworks enable the commercialization and scaling of carbon-reducing technologies while managing trade-offs between environmental and economic priorities (extending Hall et al., 2016).

--- Topic 24: Digital Economy ---
Analyzes structural inequalities in digital resource access, technological capabilities, and data capital that create differentiated entrepreneurial opportunity landscapes, examining how socio-spatial factors, power dynamics, and institutional arrangements shape participation patterns in digitally-enabled entrepreneurship (building on Kenney & Zysman, 2016).

--- Topic 25: Gender Equity in Workplaces ---
Examines policy interventions targeting persistent gender disparities in entrepreneurial work contexts, analyzing how regulatory frameworks, organizational practices, and cultural norms shape women's leadership advancement, resource access, and work-life integration within entrepreneurial ventures (extending Elam et al., 2019).
